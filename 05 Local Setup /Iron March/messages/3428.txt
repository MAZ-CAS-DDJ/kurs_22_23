
<p><span style="color:#ffffff;"><span style="font-family:Arial;"><span style="font-size:14px;">Knowledge Concealment Security (KCS)</span></span></span></p>
<p><span style="color:#ffffff;"><span style="font-family:Arial;"><span style="font-size:14px;">The KaS system is a way to improve game play by giving an extra measure of excitement, danger, and difficulty to the Shadow Play game. The idea behind this is that the more Knowledge of the “Shadow World” that the PC learns, the lower their Security gets. The Security determines how safe the PC is from rival factions such as other secret societies, government agencies, conspiracy theorists, and vigilantes.</span></span></span></p>
<p><span style="color:#ffffff;"><span style="font-family:Arial;"><span style="font-size:14px;">The only way a PC can prevent a loss of security is he successfully makes a concealment check against the perception of the PC’s, NPC’s, and Surveillance equipment. If none of these factors are in the area of the PC discovering the knowledge then the concealment check automatically succeeds. </span></span></span></p>
<p><span style="color:#ffffff;"><span style="font-family:Arial;"><span style="font-size:14px;">Knowledge</span></span></span></p>
<p><span style="color:#ffffff;"><span style="font-family:Arial;"><span style="font-size:14px;">A PC's Knowledge does not have to reflect the knowledge of the person behind the PC. The Knowledge just represents the experience in the “underworld” the PC gains such as conspiracies, cryptids, inner workings of government and/or corporate structures, and other secret societies as stated before.</span></span></span></p>
<p><span style="color:#ffffff;"><span style="font-family:Arial;"><span style="font-size:14px;">All players start out with a base knowledge of 10 out of 100 as they all start off as FBI agents presumably with college education. It is not advisable that a character's knowledge go above 65 unless they've become CEOs, Generals, Masters of a Secret Society, etc.</span></span></span></p>
<p><span style="color:#ffffff;"><span style="font-family:Arial;"><span style="font-size:14px;">To give some examples at random:</span></span></span></p>
<p><span style="color:#ffffff;"><span style="font-family:Arial;"><span style="font-size:14px;">Gray Alien – 25 knowledge</span></span></span></p>
<p><span style="color:#ffffff;"><span style="font-family:Arial;"><span style="font-size:14px;">Chupacabra – 5 knowledge</span></span></span></p>
<p><span style="color:#ffffff;"><span style="font-family:Arial;"><span style="font-size:14px;">UFO – 1-5 (depending on if the UFO is debunked as truly Unknown or naturally occurring phenomena)</span></span></span></p>
<p><span style="color:#ffffff;"><span style="font-family:Arial;"><span style="font-size:14px;">Adept level ritual of an Occult Order – 1-10 knowledge (depending on how secretive the order is)</span></span></span></p>
<p><span style="color:#ffffff;"><span style="font-family:Arial;"><span style="font-size:14px;">Government or Secret Society document about a cover up – 5-15 (depending on the body covering up the incident and the incident itself.)</span></span></span></p>
<p><span style="color:#ffffff;"><span style="font-family:Arial;"><span style="font-size:14px;">An order for your character's assassination – 15 knowledge with an additional loss of 5 security.</span></span></span></p>
<p><span style="color:#ffffff;"><span style="font-family:Arial;"><span style="font-size:14px;">Mothman – 5</span></span></span></p>
<p><span style="color:#ffffff;"><span style="font-family:Arial;"><span style="font-size:14px;">Rogue Hackers or Terrorists - 5</span></span></span></p>
<p><span style="color:#ffffff;"><span style="font-family:Arial;"><span style="font-size:14px;">Assassination – 5, 10 if the target is known, 15 if the reason and target are known.</span></span></span></p>
<p><span style="color:#ffffff;"><span style="font-family:Arial;"><span style="font-size:14px;">Unofficial conspiracy theory books, articles, documentaries, etc – 1 with a maximum of 5 i.e. When a PC reads enough he will eventually “top out” in his knowledge as these second-hand sources are never completely accurate. Raise knowledge level plus 1 per month studied no more than 5. (Time is of GM discretion.)</span></span></span></p>
<p><span style="color:#ffffff;"><span style="font-family:Arial;"><span style="font-size:14px;">Security</span></span></span></p>
<p><span style="color:#ffffff;"><span style="font-family:Arial;"><span style="font-size:14px;">Players start out with 100 security. Security measures how safe they are from opposing forces the more knowledge they gain the more security they lose. Example, seeing the Mothman gives +5 knowledge with -5 security.</span></span></span></p>
<p><span style="color:#ffffff;"><span style="font-family:Arial;"><span style="font-size:14px;">As security gets lower the risk of assassination, abduction, loss of job titles increases. The circumstances of these happenings will be up to the Game Master. At the beginning of a round the GM will roll a d100 (keeping the results secret from the players) for each PC. Security can be raised back up if witnesses are dealt with accordingly, evidence is destroyed, the PC lies low, etc.</span></span></span></p>
<p><span style="color:#ffffff;"><span style="font-family:Arial;"><span style="font-size:14px;">If only the witnesses are killed or the evidence is destroyed, recover half of the security lost from the knowledge gained. If both situations are dealt with (i.e witnesses executed AND evidence is destroyed) recover all security points. If the PC can do neither of these and has to go into hiding then  recover 1 security point per week, month, or year of hiding (GM’s discretion, based on how significant the knowledge was.) Players cannot fully recover security this way as evidence and word of mouth can be spread around. </span></span></span></p>
<p><span style="color:#ffffff;"><span style="font-family:Arial;"><span style="font-size:14px;">Here are the statistics for the security/risk levels and consequences.</span></span></span></p>
<p><span style="color:#ffffff;"><span style="font-family:Arial;"><span style="font-size:14px;">Security</span></span></span></p>
<p><span style="color:#ffffff;"><span style="font-family:Arial;"><span style="font-size:14px;">100-80</span></span></span></p>
<p><span style="color:#ffffff;"><span style="font-family:Arial;"><span style="font-size:14px;">No risk</span></span></span></p>
<p><span style="color:#ffffff;"><span style="font-family:Arial;"><span style="font-size:14px;">81-60</span></span></span></p>
<p><span style="color:#ffffff;"><span style="font-family:Arial;"><span style="font-size:14px;">Surveillance</span></span></span></p>
<p><span style="color:#ffffff;"><span style="font-family:Arial;"><span style="font-size:14px;">59-41</span></span></span></p>
<p><span style="color:#ffffff;"><span style="font-family:Arial;"><span style="font-size:14px;">Threats and harassment</span></span></span></p>
<p><span style="color:#ffffff;"><span style="font-family:Arial;"><span style="font-size:14px;">40-31</span></span></span></p>
<p><span style="color:#ffffff;"><span style="font-family:Arial;"><span style="font-size:14px;">Sabotage i.e. Bank accounts hacked into or frozen, beatings, loss of job</span></span></span></p>
<p><span style="color:#ffffff;"><span style="font-family:Arial;"><span style="font-size:14px;">30-00</span></span></span></p>
<p><span style="color:#ffffff;"><span style="font-family:Arial;"><span style="font-size:14px;">Abductions, assassination attempts of PC and/or acquaintances.</span></span></span></p>
<p><span style="color:#ffffff;"><span style="font-family:Arial;"><span style="font-size:14px;">If a D100 is rolled and the result is higher than the security level of the player, they are safe. If it is lower they face consequences based on the numbers above.</span></span></span></p>
<p><span style="color:#ffffff;"><span style="font-family:Arial;"><span style="font-size:14px;">Example</span></span></span></p>
<p><span style="color:#ffffff;"><span style="font-family:Arial;"><span style="font-size:14px;">Jack's security is 60</span></span></span></p>
<p><span style="color:#ffffff;"><span style="font-family:Arial;"><span style="font-size:14px;">Bill's security is 80</span></span></span></p>
<p><span style="color:#ffffff;"><span style="font-family:Arial;"><span style="font-size:14px;">John's security is 40</span></span></span></p>
<p><span style="color:#ffffff;"><span style="font-family:Arial;"><span style="font-size:14px;">Tom's security is 19</span></span></span></p>
<p><span style="color:#ffffff;"><span style="font-family:Arial;"><span style="font-size:14px;">The game master rolls the following results for the players on their security checks.</span></span></span></p>
<p><span style="color:#ffffff;"><span style="font-family:Arial;"><span style="font-size:14px;">64 -Jack is safe</span></span></span></p>
<p><span style="color:#ffffff;"><span style="font-family:Arial;"><span style="font-size:14px;">90 -Bill safe</span></span></span></p>
<p><span style="color:#ffffff;"><span style="font-family:Arial;"><span style="font-size:14px;">50 -John is safe</span></span></span></p>
<p><span style="color:#ffffff;"><span style="font-family:Arial;"><span style="font-size:14px;">10 -Tom's security is greater than 10 (at 19) therefore he would be wise to limit his time out in public and check his vehicle for explosives rigged to it. The Gamemaster decides that an unseen assassin is perched on a roof with a scoped mosin nagant, aiming at Tom's character.</span></span></span></p>
<p><span style="color:#ffffff;"><span style="font-family:Arial;"><span style="font-size:14px;">Now, if an assassination, abduction, or any or threat is about to befall the character the GM will have the threatened player make a security check of his own. If the PC rolls lower than his security level he spots the threat and can react accordingly.</span></span></span></p>
<p><span style="color:#ffffff;"><span style="font-family:Arial;"><span style="font-size:14px;">Example.</span></span></span></p>
<p><span style="color:#ffffff;"><span style="font-family:Arial;"><span style="font-size:14px;">Tom rolls a miraculous 06 on his d100, he spots the assassin just in time and a combat round begins.</span></span></span></p>
