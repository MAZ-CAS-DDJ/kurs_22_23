
<p>In the Meiji Constitution (effective from establishment until 1947) the Emperor was supreme leader of the country but in practice this was not the case, for His Majesty would leave most of the decisions to the appropriate departments. </p>
<p> </p>
<p>If Japan had taken an active part in Asian nationalism (unthinkable to the reactionary leadership because the basis of Jap foreign policy was the alliance with the UK) it would have put itself in grave danger but at the same time may have been able to liberate Asia much faster and without the unpleasant aftertaste of colonialism--while keeping Japan's premier position in Asia as the "first liberator".</p>
