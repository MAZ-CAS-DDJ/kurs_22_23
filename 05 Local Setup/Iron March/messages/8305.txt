
<p>It's cool that you're interested. The new-age stuff can be annoying, but here's my list of worthwhile books. </p>
<p> </p>
<p>The Prose Edda, the book of all books.</p>
<p> </p>
<p><a href="http://www.amazon.com/Prose-Edda-Mythology-Penguin-Classics/dp/0140447555/ref=sr_1_2?ie=UTF8&amp;qid=1423752888&amp;sr=8-2&amp;keywords=eddas" rel="external nofollow">http://www.amazon.com/Prose-Edda-Mythology-Penguin-Classics/dp/0140447555/ref=sr_1_2?ie=UTF8&amp;qid=1423752888&amp;sr=8-2&amp;keywords=eddas</a></p>
<p> </p>
<p>Guido von List, who was responsible for the revival of heathenism and heavily influenced early NS ideology. </p>
<p> </p>
<p><a href="http://www.amazon.com/Secret-Runes-Guido-von-List/dp/0892812079/ref=sr_1_1?ie=UTF8&amp;qid=1423752864&amp;sr=8-1&amp;keywords=guido+von+list" rel="external nofollow">http://www.amazon.com/Secret-Runes-Guido-von-List/dp/0892812079/ref=sr_1_1?ie=UTF8&amp;qid=1423752864&amp;sr=8-1&amp;keywords=guido+von+list</a></p>
<p> </p>
<p>"Edred Thorsson", or Stephen Flowers. Solid runeology based on List's armanen system. </p>
<p><a href="http://www.amazon.com/Futhark-Handbook-Magic-Edred-Thorsson/dp/0877285489/ref=sr_1_1?ie=UTF8&amp;qid=1423752997&amp;sr=8-1&amp;keywords=edred+thorsson" rel="external nofollow">http://www.amazon.com/Futhark-Handbook-Magic-Edred-Thorsson/dp/0877285489/ref=sr_1_1?ie=UTF8&amp;qid=1423752997&amp;sr=8-1&amp;keywords=edred+thorsson</a></p>
<p><a href="http://www.amazon.com/ALU-Advanced-Guide-Operative-Runology/dp/1578635268/ref=sr_1_4?ie=UTF8&amp;qid=1423752997&amp;sr=8-4&amp;keywords=edred+thorsson" rel="external nofollow">http://www.amazon.com/ALU-Advanced-Guide-Operative-Runology/dp/1578635268/ref=sr_1_4?ie=UTF8&amp;qid=1423752997&amp;sr=8-4&amp;keywords=edred+thorsson</a></p>
<p> </p>
<p>These are not a bad books either, but take them with a grain of salt. Personally I don't engage in most of the rituals described.</p>
<p><a href="http://www.amazon.com/Our-Troth-History-Kveldulf-Gundarsson/dp/1419635980/ref=pd_sim_b_5?ie=UTF8&amp;refRID=15PVZ0M1M3G4JYPXTJ47" rel="external nofollow">http://www.amazon.com/Our-Troth-History-Kveldulf-Gundarsson/dp/1419635980/ref=pd_sim_b_5?ie=UTF8&amp;refRID=15PVZ0M1M3G4JYPXTJ47</a></p>
<p><a href="http://www.amazon.com/A-Practical-Heathens-Guide-Asatru/dp/0738733873/ref=pd_sim_b_2?ie=UTF8&amp;refRID=1XTA2BGHFZYPXCQRA14S" rel="external nofollow">http://www.amazon.com/A-Practical-Heathens-Guide-Asatru/dp/0738733873/ref=pd_sim_b_2?ie=UTF8&amp;refRID=1XTA2BGHFZYPXCQRA14S</a></p>
<p> </p>
<p>Post 1945 heathen authors usually have a slight anti-NS tone, but it isn't usually very sincere. It's more designed to inoculate the religion from criticism - of all religions, heathenism is the most intertwined with NS ideology.  </p>
<p> </p>
<p>If you don't want to get any of these, I would recommend reading the Havamal. It's free online.</p>
<p><a href="http://www.pitt.edu/~dash/havamal.html" rel="external nofollow">http://www.pitt.edu/~dash/havamal.html</a></p>
